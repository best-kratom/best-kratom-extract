## What Are Kratom Extracts?
Kratom extracts are concentrated forms of the kratom plant. They're made by pulling the active alkaloids, primarily mitragynine and 7-hydroxymitragynine, out of dried kratom leaves and concentrating them into a smaller, more potent product.

**How Extracts Differ from Regular Kratom:** Regular kratom powder has a mitragynine concentration of roughly 0.5-2%. Extracts can reach 45% or higher, up to 90 times more potent gram-for-gram.

**Why Extracts Are More Potent:** The extraction process removes most plant fiber and inactive material, leaving a dense concentration of alkaloids. Smaller amounts produce stronger effects.

**Types of Extracts - 10x, 20x, Liquid Shots:** 10x extract means the final product is 10 times stronger than regular leaf powder. 20x is 20 times stronger. Liquid shots are pre-measured, ready-to-drink formats with a standardized milligram count per bottle.

**How Are Kratom Extracts Made:** Leaves are ground, soaked in a food-grade solvent like ethanol, then strained and filtered repeatedly. The solvent evaporates, leaving a concentrated extract. Some brands use cold extraction to preserve delicate alkaloids. The result is a liquid, powder, or resin, depending on the process.

## Possible Side Effects and Safety Tips

Kratom extracts are potent. Using them without care can lead to side effects. Here's what to watch for and how to stay safe:

**Nausea:** The most common side effect, especially at higher doses or for new users. Take extracts with food to reduce their effects.

**Dizziness:** High doses can cause lightheadedness. Start small and stay seated when trying a new product for the first time.

**Dependency Risk:** Regular daily use can lead to tolerance and physical dependence. Rotate strains and take regular breaks to avoid this.

**Safe Usage Tips:** Start with the smallest serving. Do not combine kratom with alcohol, prescription opioids, or sedatives. Stay hydrated. Never drive after a strong dose.

**Who Should Avoid Kratom Extracts:** Pregnant women, people with liver conditions, those on prescription medications, and anyone under 21 should avoid kratom extracts. Always consult a doctor before use.
